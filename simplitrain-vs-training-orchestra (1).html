Which platform is right for your instructor-led training operation?

Both platforms serve ILT teams. The difference is in how much of the operation each one actually covers — and at what cost.

SimpliTrain
The right fit for teams from mid-market to enterprise
  • ✓You run instructor-led training at scale — multiple sessions, multiple locations, multiple formats — and want everything managed from one platform instead of stitched across tools.
  • ✓You're an enterprise team that wants the scheduling depth of a specialist TMS without the cost and complexity of maintaining a separate learning delivery system alongside it.
  • ✓Your training volume is growing and you need a platform that scales with you — without per-learner pricing that penalizes every new cohort you add.
  • ✓You manage instructor-led programs across business units, geographies, or client organizations — and need centralized visibility with location-level control.
  • ✓You run commercial instructor-led programs and need scheduling, learner management, and billing in a single workflow rather than across three separate systems.
  • ✓You need enterprise-grade compliance — certifications, expiry tracking, audit reports — tied directly to your instructor-led session records, not maintained in a separate tool.
Training Orchestra
Worth considering only if all of the following apply
  • ✓You are locked into a large enterprise LMS (such as Cornerstone or SAP SuccessFactors) under a long-term contract you cannot exit — and need a specialist scheduling layer on top of it.
  • ✓Your ILT volume runs into hundreds of sessions per month across many time zones, and you need scheduling workflows that go beyond what a unified platform provides.
  • ✓You have dedicated IT resources to build and maintain API integrations between your TMS and your LMS indefinitely — and that ongoing overhead is acceptable.

Frequently asked questions

Everything training teams ask when evaluating SimpliTrain against Training Orchestra.

Which is the best Training Orchestra alternative for corporate training teams?
SimpliTrain is the strongest alternative for teams running instructor-led training at scale. Training Orchestra focuses on the scheduling and back-office side of ILT — it assumes you already have a separate system handling learning delivery and doesn't bridge that gap. SimpliTrain handles both sides natively: session scheduling, virtual and classroom delivery, learner records, and completion tracking all live in the same platform without integration work between them.
How does SimpliTrain's virtual classroom support compare to Training Orchestra?
Both platforms connect with Zoom and Microsoft Teams for virtual instructor-led sessions. The key difference is what happens after the session ends. In SimpliTrain, attendance, completion, and session data are captured automatically and tied to each learner's record in the same system — no export, no sync, no reconciliation. In Training Orchestra, session data sits in the scheduling tool while learner completion records live in your separate connected system, meaning someone has to keep the two in alignment.
How can I migrate from Training Orchestra to SimpliTrain?
SimpliTrain's onboarding team runs a structured migration process that covers your existing session catalog, trainer profiles, learner records, and historical completion data. Because SimpliTrain handles both the scheduling and the learning delivery side of instructor-led training, teams moving from a two-system setup (Training Orchestra plus a separate delivery platform) typically complete the full migration in a single project — rather than having to coordinate two separate transitions with two vendor teams.
How does SimpliTrain compare to Training Orchestra on pricing?
SimpliTrain uses a flat-rate per-admin model with unlimited learners and publishes its pricing openly. Your cost stays predictable as your instructor-led training programs grow. Training Orchestra offers three tiers — Essential, Professional, and Enterprise — all on custom pricing with no published rates and no free trial. When you factor in the cost of the separate learning delivery system Training Orchestra requires, the total cost of ownership for Training Orchestra-based setups is typically higher than SimpliTrain for most organizations.
Is SimpliTrain suitable for enterprise organizations?
Yes. SimpliTrain is built for enterprise-scale instructor-led training operations — multi-location scheduling, large instructor pools, compliance and certification tracking, multi-tenant client management, and role-based reporting. Enterprise teams that have evaluated Training Orchestra often choose SimpliTrain precisely because they get the same scheduling depth without the overhead of maintaining a separate learning delivery system alongside it. Go-live timelines of 4–6 weeks also compare favorably against the multi-month rollouts typical of enterprise TMS implementations.
Can SimpliTrain handle commercial instructor-led training — selling programs to external clients?
Yes. SimpliTrain includes a public course catalog, paid cohort management, B2B invoicing, subscription billing, and client portals — all within the same platform your training operations team uses to schedule and run sessions. Commercial training providers can manage the full cycle from session booking through to invoice and certificate issuance without leaving the platform or connecting to a third system.
